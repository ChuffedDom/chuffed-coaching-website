## Purpose
The aim is to create and provide the means for someone in tech to at least work of something to find a bit of financial freedom in shitty times.

## Vision
A means of empowering tech folks to build a product and effectively get it into people's hands to either have a main source of income or something shit hot to put into a CV/help with a personal brand.

## Goals
- I want to test something in the market and see what type of adoption can happen.
- I need to productise some of the offerings of Chuffed to gain larger reach for those who cannot partake in the high ticket offering

## Problem
As someone in tech (particular in software engineering) I want to build, ship, and monetise a product to an extent to gain me some financial freedom in the turbulent time of destabilised economies and a tech job market that is somewhat in the toilet.

However, whilst I have the technical skills to build and maintain a piece of software, I have not developed the human skill need to make product decisions, put together a go-to-market strategy, or effectively do brand awareness (and honestly not entirely sure what these disciplines even are).

Hence it has led me to not talk to enough people, test ideas and implementation in the market, and sit on my hands when it comes to any kind of audience building.

Therefore, I feel lost on how to get started and end up just binging on hack, tricks, and shortcuts to try and find some version of market-fit to only not gain or learn anything to help me move forward.

## Hypothesis

If Chuffed has a digital product that fills in the blanks of everything outside of writing code that is require to start the process of launched a monetised product and positioning it as a self-guided learning experience and help them with accountability, then people who are getting caught up in the troubled waters of tech could be compelled to take it onboard as part of their journey should everything go tits up.

## Proposal
### T-minus 180
An online course to help folks build a roadmap for the next 180 days so at the end they have:
- A clear idea of a problem to solve and how to solve it (strong case for an app idea)
- A non-cringe marketing plan that doesn't involving dancing on camera
- Order of operations for marketing, planning, and development

People can sign up for £100 and get access to a course that will teach them to concepts behind running a business based around building software.

The course will be delivered as a weekly email that has one routine for the week that is something to do every day (10-15mins) and 3 tasks to complete. The concepts and principles behind the tasks will be presented in 15min talk and designed to teach concepts in Product, Go-to-Market, and Brand Awareness over 6-month whilst actually doing it with the intention that after 180 days they have the tools needed to know exactly what to do to have a portfolio career.

### Outline for 6 months

The course is structured into three distinct phases, broken down into weekly routines and tasks to move from "unready" to "founder-ready."

#### Phase 1: In it for the Long Game (Days 1-60)
> The best time to plant a tree was long ago, the second best time is now.

**Objectives:** Validate a persona or archetype, plant long-term seeds (SEO/Social), and master storytelling.

- **Week 1: `whoami`** - Identify the weakest link in your "Chain" (GTM, Product, Branding, etc.) // a loose version of SWOT but less wanky.
- **Week 2: Speculative Persona** - Draft the hypothesis of who you think has a problem, getting people to a place of committing to one step "who am I building for?" // ideally to focus on what type of person is the easiest to get in front of.
- **Week 3: Brand Consistency** - Secure domain, handles, and basic "Build in Public" landing page. // make sure of consistent branding today because this can bite you in the arse later down the line.
- **Week 4: Finding your Voice** - Establish a weekly publishing rhythm to start the 6-month trust-building clock. // go-to-market can start before the product by building an audience and clear message for them. Setting the creator up as the perfect person to solve their problems, in their eyes
- **Week 5: Existing in the Right Space** - Find 3 digital communities where your the person you are going to build for hangs out. // The act of getting in front of people to understand their problems, being able to communicate your brand, and to eventually pitch your product is all of the same activities; being in the space of the people who are most likely to become your users.
- **Week 6: The Speculation Loop** - Start "Deep Listening" and asking esoteric questions to validate pain. // Getting to a point where ask if esoteric question starts to yield predictable specific answers.
- **Week 7: Refinement** - Update your speculative persona based on community feedback. // Open up a feedback loop of gaining insights, updating the persona, generating questions, gaining more insights, etc.
- **Week 8: Community Contribution** - Build reputation as a "useful peer" by providing value without selling. // Having a clear pipeline of means of delivering value, whether that be through software, in-person events, online events, etc.

#### Phase 2: Shipping for Validation (Days 61-120)
> The only validation that matters is building something, putting it in people's hands and having them take actions to purchase.

**Objective:** Ship professional "micro-solutions" to verify market interest and test conversion.

- **Week 9: Storytelling Framework** - Learn the "Pain-Agitate-Solve" model for founder-marketing. // Build up a storytelling to an extent where you can articulate the problem back to the personal experience in it and they can see the empathy that you have for them in that state.
- **Week 10: Expressing Pain** - Write 5 pieces of content that describe the persons pain better than they can. // Steering away from the common trap which is to focus messaging around solutions and what people do and towards expressing the pain they face.
- **Week 11: Controlled Space Strategy** - Design your "Island" (Slack circle, niche newsletter, or Discord). // Existing in other people's spaces can only get you so far so you need to build an island. But that works well because people by their very nature will congregate around the same problem.
- **Week 12: Launching the Space** - Onboard your first 10-20 "High-Intent" people from your research phase. // From the point of being able to articulate a problem well enough, people are going to start to gravitate towards you and almost beg you for the solution.
- **Week 13: Dip the Toe** - Plan a 2-hour build (template, script, or guide) that solves 1% of the pain. // Now is a point to start testing the market. Some results will take six to eight weeks in order to get insights. So starting at this point is very important.
- **Week 14: Shipping #1** - Release your first micro-asset to your controlled space and measure signals. // This is all about testing the market with real, tangible things in the world.
- **Week 15: Shipping #2** - Release a different format (e.g., switching from a PDF to a Notion tool). // Shifting the medium helps to understand that the delivery is not connected to the value.
- **Week 16: Signal Analysis** - Review hand-raisers. Identify where attention is clustering. // A clear focus away from vanity metrics and quantitative data to important signals that tell you a lot about your direction and how to position.

#### Phase 3: Stacking the Odds & Launch (Days 121-180)
> """You miss 100% of the shots you don't take." — Wayne Gretzky" — Micheal Scott

**Objective:** Solve a "bleeding neck" problem, build a scoped MVP, and launch to your controlled space.

- **Week 17: Pitching the Invisible** - Craft a high-level promise for a product that doesn't exist yet (Result + Persona + Timeframe) to see if anyone actually wants it. // Testing various value propositions and listening to the signal coming back.
- **Week 18: Testing for "How do I get it?"** - Build a high-signal landing page to see if your pitch makes people want to know how to buy it before you build a single line of code. // The true test of your hypothesis and if you can compel your persona.
- **Week 19: Rapid Pivot Loop** - If the pitch fails to land, pivot quickly and swiftly; keep testing different hooks until you find what the market actually demands. // Like fighting a Dark Souls boss you need to try different load outs and strategies to find out what works.
- **Week 20: High-Signal Commitment** - Implement pre-commitments (micro-payments or deep surveys). // Breakdown the User Flows that HAVE TO be completed in order to deliver value.
- **Week 21: The Bleeding Neck Filter** - Finalise the ONE urgent and impactful problem for the first build. // Generate interests in what people want to pay for, and not what people would quite like to use or think; "oh that is a bit cool".
- **Week 22: Kill your darlings** - Identify the "Elegant Solution" and kill 80% of "nice-to-have" features. // Build up to the scale of your validation. Anything above it is nothing but risk.
- **Week 23: Build Sprint Week 1** - Implement the core functionality. // And now we start to do "build in public" shift in marketing.
- **Week 24: Build Sprint Week 2** - Onboarding, UI, and internal feedback loops. // introduce a sense of urgency and exclusivity as you build.
- **Week 25: Build Sprint Week 3** - Final polish and payment integration. // Bring on early adopters who are most likely to be your evangelists.
- **Week 26: Launch Week** - Take the product to your controlled space for the initial monetisation push. // It's go time.

### The Island: Community & Movement
Building in public is hard; building in isolation is nearly impossible. As part of T-180, you aren't just joining a course; you are joining a **movement** of independent developers reclaiming their agency.

#### The Discord Accountability Space
To ensure you actually reach Day 180, we maintain a dedicated Discord server designed for:
- Weekly check-ins to share progress, blockers, and wins. No one builds in a vacuum and we all need to get shit done.
- A living library of every deep-dive video, template, script, and link mentioned in the course, categorised by week and phase.
- Get "Useful Peer" reviews on your speculative personas, landing pages, and micro-assets before they go live.
- Maybe virtual co-working??

This space is your "Island" (needs a better name) a controlled environment away from the noise of general social media where you can focus on the work that actually leads to a monetised product.
